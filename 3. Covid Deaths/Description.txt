The COVID‑19 pandemic, also known as the coronavirus pandemic, is an ongoing pandemic of coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID‑19) caused by severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 (SARS CoV 2).
The disease was first identified in December 2019 in Wuhan, China.
The World Health Organization declared the outbreak a Public Health Emergency of International Concern on 30 January 2020 and a pandemic on 11 March.
As of 12 September 2020, more than 28.6 million cases have been reported in more than 188 countries and territories, resulting in more than 917,000 deaths; more than 19.2 million people have recovered.
